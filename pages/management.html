Ethics & Governance

This section discusses the ethical considerations surrounding the design and deployment of the Hidden In Plain Sight steganographic messaging system. Because the application enables covert communication, it is important to address issues such as privacy, responsible use, system limitations, and research ethics.

Ethics Statement

Privacy & Information Protection

Hidden In Plain Sight embeds messages inside JPEG images to allow covert communication. Since hidden information may be sensitive, the application is designed so that plaintext messages are not permanently stored. Messages exist only temporarily during the embedding or extraction process and are discarded immediately afterward.

Additionally, images may contain metadata such as location information or timestamps. While the algorithm modifies JPEG compression coefficients, developers must remain aware that metadata could potentially reveal unintended information.

Responsible Use

Steganographic communication can support legitimate use cases such as secure intelligence transmission or privacy protection in monitored environments. However, similar techniques could also be misused.

Because of this dual-use nature, the project documentation clearly defines the intended purpose and limitations of the system and avoids making misleading claims about the technology.

System Reliability

Software used in sensitive communication environments must behave reliably. If an application fails during message embedding or extraction, information could be lost or exposed.

The system is therefore designed to reject invalid inputs, handle errors safely, and avoid storing hidden messages after they are processed.

Transparency & Limitations

This project does not claim to produce undetectable steganography. Advanced steganalysis techniques may still detect patterns that suggest hidden data. The system aims to reduce detectability rather than guarantee perfect secrecy.

Documenting these limitations helps ensure that users understand the realistic capabilities of the technology.

IRB / CITI Status

This project does not involve human subject research or the collection of personal data from participants. Testing is performed using developer generated datasets and non-personal images.

Because of this, formal IRB approval is not currently required.

Status: Not Required (No Human Subject Research)
